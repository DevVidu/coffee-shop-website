Espresso

A concentrated shot of coffee brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee beans. It's characterized by its strong, bold flavor and is served in a small demitasse cup. Espresso forms the base for various other coffee beverages.


Macchiato

Espresso "stained" with a dollop of steamed milk or milk foam. The word "macchiato" means "stained" or "marked" in Italian, indicating the small amount of milk added to the espresso. It balances the intensity of espresso with a touch of creaminess.


Cappuccino

Equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam, resulting in a creamy and balanced coffee drink. It typically has a strong espresso flavor, a layer of frothy milk foam, and a silky texture from the steamed milk.


Latte

Espresso with steamed milk and a small layer of milk foam, often customizable with flavored syrups. Lattes have a higher milk-to-espresso ratio compared to cappuccinos, resulting in a creamier and smoother taste. They're often served in larger cups or glasses.


Mocha

A latte with added chocolate syrup or cocoa powder, creating a chocolate-flavored coffee drink. Mochas blend the richness of chocolate with the creaminess of a latte, offering a delightful combination of coffee and chocolate flavors.
